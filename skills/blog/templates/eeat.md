# Template: E-E-A-T Optimized Blog Post

**Description:** High-authority blog post structured for Google's E-E-A-T framework (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) and AI citation readiness. Every section embeds demonstrable trust signals — named authorship, primary-source citations, first-hand experience markers, visible freshness, and answer-first formatting. Optimized for the December 2025 Core Update which extended E-E-A-T requirements beyond YMYL to all competitive queries.

**Target Word Count:** 2,000-3,500 words

---

## When to Use This Template

- **Content Goal:** Establish topical authority and demonstrate credibility for competitive queries
- **Search Intent:** Informational (users want a trusted, verifiable answer)
- **Funnel Stage:** Top-of-funnel awareness and mid-funnel comparison
- **Use When:**
  - The topic has YMYL implications (health, finance, safety, career)
  - Your author has verifiable credentials or direct experience in the topic
  - You need to differentiate from commodity content that rehashes surface-level info
  - The query is competitive and E-E-A-T signals will be a ranking differentiator
  - You want the post cited by AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AIO

---

## Section-by-Section Structure

---

### Author Attribution

Place this immediately after the title, before the TL;DR. Non-negotiable for any content targeting E-E-A-T scoring.

**Format:**
```
**By [Author Full Name]**
[Credential or role, 5-10 words]. *Last updated: [YYYY-MM-DD]*
```

**Guidelines:**
- Use a real, named author — never "Admin," "Team," or anonymous
- The credential must be specific to this topic (e.g., "Registered Dietitian" not "Content Writer")
- Link the author name to a dedicated Author Page on your site with:
  - Real headshot photo (not stock, not logo, not avatar)
  - 2-3 sentence bio with specific credentials and relevant experience
  - Link to LinkedIn or a verifiable professional profile
  - Person Schema markup (JSON-LD) linking to your Organization

If the author lacks direct experience, pair them with a named subject-matter reviewer and credit both:

```
**By [Author Full Name]** · *Reviewed by [Reviewer Full Name], [Credential]*
*Last updated: [YYYY-MM-DD]*
```

---

### Title

**Format:** "[Question or How-To Format] in [Year]" or "[Definitive Statement]: [Topic] in [Year]"

**Guidelines:**
- Include primary keyword naturally in the first 50 characters
- Append the year to signal freshness and improve CTR
- Keep under 60 characters for full SERP display
- Avoid clickbait — the title must accurately reflect what the post delivers

```
# [Question-Format Title Including Primary Keyword] in [Year]
```

---

### TL;DR / Key Takeaways Box (40-60 words)

Place before the introduction. This is the first body content a reader (and AI crawler) encounters — it must deliver immediate value.

```
> **Key Takeaways**
> - [Core finding with specific statistic and named source]
> - [Second key insight or recommendation]
> - [Third actionable takeaway based on experience]
```

**Requirements:**
- 3-5 bullet points, 40-60 words total
- Must be self-contained — understandable without reading the article
- Bullet 1 MUST contain a verifiable statistic with source name and year
- Bullet 2 MUST contain an experience signal ("we found," "I tested," "our analysis shows")
- Bullet 3 SHOULD contain a recommendation the reader can act on immediately
- The summary box itself is an E-E-A-T trust signal — it shows respect for the reader's time

### Introduction (150-250 words)

[ANSWER-FIRST] The first 2-3 sentences must deliver a direct, expert-level answer to the query the post targets. Burying the lead is the #1 E-E-A-T mistake.

**Structure:**
1. **Direct answer** (2-3 sentences): Answer the core question immediately. A reader who reads only these sentences should get the answer they came for.
   - [STAT: current-year statistic that grounds the answer]
2. **Why this matters now** (2-3 sentences): Establish recency and relevance. What has changed in the last 12-18 months?
3. **What this article covers** (2-3 sentences): Set scope and expectations.
4. **Who this is for** (1 sentence): Define the target reader so they self-select.
5. **Credibility anchor** (1 sentence): One specific signal that shows why this author/site should be trusted on this topic.

[EXPERIENCE: first-hand signal] Embed a brief first-person experience statement — "In my [N] years working with [topic]," "When I tested [X], I found," "Our team analyzed" — within the introduction. This is the single highest-weight E-E-A-T signal Google evaluates in 2026.

[TRUST: cite primary source] Include a citation to a primary source (peer-reviewed study, official statistics, authoritative report) within the introduction.

[SCHEMA: Article + Author] Ensure Article schema with author attribution is present in the page JSON-LD.

### H2: [Question-Format Sub-Topic] (250-400 words)

[ANSWER-FIRST] Open with a 40-60 word paragraph containing a specific statistic with source attribution and a direct answer to the heading's implicit question.

**Structure:**
1. **Answer-first paragraph** (40-60 words): Stat + source + direct answer
   - [STAT: specific data point with year, source name, and URL]
2. **Experience signal** (50-100 words): First-person evidence — what happened when you did this, tested this, or encountered this
   - [EXPERIENCE: specific detail only someone who has done this would know]
   - Weak: "Experts recommend tracking this metric"
   - Strong: "When I tracked this metric across 12 campaigns, I found that the first-week reading predicted 83% of the final outcome"
3. **Depth marker** (50-75 words): An explanation that goes beyond surface level. Anticipate a follow-up question the reader would have and answer it proactively.
4. **Transition** (1-2 sentences): Bridge to the next section

[INFO-GAIN: unique analysis or original data] This section MUST contain at least one original observation, proprietary data point, or unique analysis that cannot be found on competitor pages. Without this, the section is commodity content.

[TRUST: primary source] Cite the original research, not a blog post about the research. A citation chain of "we read the actual study" signals higher E-E-A-T.

[INTERNAL-LINK] Link to a relevant supporting article or deeper dive within your site. Use descriptive anchor text.

### H2: [Question-Format Sub-Topic] (250-400 words)

[ANSWER-FIRST] Stat + source + direct answer in the opening 40-60 words.

**Structure:**
1. **Answer-first paragraph** (40-60 words)
2. **Evidence block** (100-150 words): Present data in a structured format that AI systems can parse
   - [VISUAL: chart-type] Generate a chart via blog-chart sub-skill (bar, lollipop, grouped bar, or line chart)
   - OR use a markdown comparison table — AI reads tables better than images

   ```
   | Metric | [Approach A] | [Approach B] |
   |--------|-------------|-------------|
   | [Metric 1] | [Data] | [Data] |
   | [Metric 2] | [Data] | [Data] |
   | [Metric 3] | [Data] | [Data] |
   ```

3. **Analysis** (50-75 words): What the data means. This is where expertise shows — anyone can copy a number, but explaining why it matters requires genuine understanding.
4. **Practical application** (50-75 words): How the reader should apply this information

[STAT: supporting statistic] Include at least one statistic with a primary-source citation per H2.

[EXPERIENCE: specific detail] Include a concrete, verifiable detail that only comes from direct involvement — a specific tool version, a date something happened, a specific number from your own tracking.

[INTERNAL-LINK] Link to a case study, tool guide, or supporting article in your cluster.

### H2: [Statement-Format Section for Variety] (250-350 words)

[ANSWER-FIRST] Direct opening with sourced claim.

Not every H2 needs to be a question. Alternate question-format H2s with statement-format H2s to maintain natural reading rhythm.

**Structure:**
1. **Claim + evidence** (40-60 words): State the finding, then back it immediately
2. **How this was determined** (50-75 words): Briefly describe methodology — this is a trust signal
   - "To verify this, we analyzed [N] data points from [source] spanning [time period]"
   - "I tested this by [specific method] over [timeframe]"
3. **Real-world example** (75-100 words): A specific case, scenario, or narrative
   - [INFO-GAIN: personal experience] First-hand example with specific, verifiable details
4. **Reader implication** (50-75 words): What this means for the reader's situation
5. **Limitation or nuance** (25-50 words): Show expertise by acknowledging where this does not apply or what the exceptions are

[TRUST: transparency] If there are limitations or conflicting evidence, acknowledge them. Google's QRG specifically rewards content that presents a complete picture including nuance.

[IMAGE] Relevant image with descriptive alt text. Prefer original screenshots or photos over stock photography — original media is a trust signal.

**Guidelines:**

- Each answer: 40-80 words, concise and direct
- The first sentence of each answer must be a standalone direct answer (featured snippet format)
- Include a [STAT: supporting statistic] in at least 3 answers
- Cover a range: basic definitions, practical how-tos, comparisons, and common misconceptions
- Questions should be conversational queries the body sections do not directly answer — this prevents content overlap

## Schema Requirements

Every E-E-A-T post must include these JSON-LD blocks:

1. **Article schema** — with `author` reference (Person), `datePublished`, `dateModified`, `image`, `description`, `mainEntityOfPage`
2. **Person schema** — on the author page, with `sameAs` linking to LinkedIn, ORCID, or other verifiable profiles
3. **Organization schema** — on the site, with `sameAs` for social profiles, contact info, logo
4. **FAQPage schema** — for the FAQ section (if present)
5. **BreadcrumbList schema** — for navigation context

The `sameAs` property linking authors and brand to external profiles (LinkedIn, Wikipedia, ORCID) is the single highest-leverage schema element for E-E-A-T scoring. This cannot be faked — Google verifies these links.

## Pre-Publish E-E-A-T Checklist

### Trust (highest priority — failure here nullifies all other signals)
- [ ] Named author with real credentials visible at top of post
- [ ] Author links to a dedicated Author Page with Person Schema
- [ ] All statistics cite primary sources (original research, not blog posts about research)
- [ ] "Last updated" date is present and reflects genuine content review (not cosmetic date bumps)
- [ ] Privacy policy, terms of service, and contact information are linked from the page
- [ ] Affiliate or commercial relationships are disclosed (FTC compliance = trust signal)
- [ ] HTTPS with valid SSL — non-negotiable baseline
- [ ] No deceptive ads, interstitials, or dark patterns

### Experience (highest-weight signal in December 2025 Core Update)
- [ ] At least one first-person experience marker in the introduction
- [ ] At least 3 [EXPERIENCE] markers distributed across body sections
- [ ] Each experience marker contains a specific, verifiable detail (date, version, quantity, tool name)
- [ ] No stock photography as hero/author images — use original or properly licensed real images
- [ ] "How we tested" or methodology note present (even if brief)

### Expertise
- [ ] Every H2 opens with an answer-first paragraph containing a stat + source
- [ ] Content anticipates and answers at least one follow-up question per section
- [ ] At least one acknowledgement of nuance, limitation, or exception
- [ ] Sources are primary (peer-reviewed, official statistics, original reports) not secondary
- [ ] No fabricated or uncited statistics
- [ ] Heading hierarchy is clean: H1 → H2 → H3 (no skipped levels)

### Authority
- [ ] At least 2 citations to well-recognized authoritative sources in the field
- [ ] Internal links to 2-4 related articles on your site (topic cluster signal)
- [ ] Outbound links to trusted, relevant external sources
- [ ] Author has verifiable external profiles (LinkedIn, Google Scholar, ORCID, industry directory)
- [ ] Organization About page is complete with real people, real contact info

### Freshness
- [ ] Year is in the title (e.g., "in 2026")
- [ ] All statistics are from the last 12-18 months
- [ ] "Last updated" date reflects a genuine content review (not just a date change)
- [ ] Any claims about "current" or "recent" phenomena include a specific time reference

### AI Citation Readiness (GEO/AEO)
- [ ] TL;DR box at top is self-contained with stat + source
- [ ] FAQ answers start with a direct sentence (standalone, no preamble)
- [ ] Data is presented in parseable formats (tables, lists, explicit definitions)
- [ ] Each H2 section is independently quotable (contains claim + evidence + source)
- [ ] No important information is embedded only in images — AI cannot read image text reliably
